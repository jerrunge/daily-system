The work
Twenty years in rooms where the stakes were real and in the field where it all comes to life. Big operations and teams with real consequences. With the human at the center of it all.

Engaged as President of a private education company, I understand the leadership requirements to transform a business when P&L management, organizational culture, and merging brands are at play.

I am not a consultant speaking from a distance. I have been in the room. I know what it costs.

Why Fortify exists
A whole-human centered approach.

People are pretty incredible if you think about it. Each of us has so much that makes up the whole. One of the things that has always frustrated me, or at least caused me to wonder, is why traditional coaching only reaches some of it.

As I experienced my own summits and valleys through my career, and have seen others do the same, help often consisted of a quick tip, a reference, or maybe even some mentorship. About how to look, say, or be a little better. But often the concept of the entire person moving forward has been left behind.

Science and research show that it is actually incredibly difficult, if not impossible, to extrapolate one, two, or even three parts of a person, work on those, and believe everything else will fall into place. And many of us who have done that type of work feel good in the moment and later wonder: is it really us? Are we okay? Do we, as a full being, belong?

Fortify was built on deep research to connect the entire person. Focusing on things that are sometimes uncomfortable, sometimes considered sensitive, but all crucial in helping a person move forward. How we feel under our clothes, at the gym, in the bedroom, on the beach, and in the boardroom are all incredibly interconnected. Working on all dimensions of self allows us, more than anything else, to see our incredible gift of who we already are and the possibility of who our entire person can be.
