Somewhere in all of it, I also got very good at performing the version of me that the room needed, watching myself and others become less sure of the versions that were actually there.

The person
I live in Pacifica, California, with my partner David and our Bernedoodle, Cooper. The Pacific is right there. Most mornings, so are we.

I have three adult kids. Maddy is 21 and one of the most capable humans I know. She has AuDHD and has taught me more about how systems fail people than any leadership book ever has. Karston is 23 and lives in San Francisco. Jeremiah is 19 and in Indiana. I love them in the particular way you love people who show you exactly who you are.

I am gay. I spent a good portion of my career in rooms that did not know that about me — not because I was hiding, but because I was performing. There is a difference. The distinction cost me something. It is also part of why Fortify goes where most coaching will not.

I have ADHD, depression, and anxiety. I lift weights, walk Cooper at 7am, and try to get to the gym before 4:30. I am actively learning to ask for help from the people who would do anything for me. That is harder than it sounds.

I write. Not to perform. To figure out what I actually think. The essays and poems on this site are the closest thing to a journal I have ever made public. If something stops you, good.

The work
Twenty years in rooms where the stakes were real. Apple. GoPuff. Spring Education. Big operations, big teams, real consequences.

I was a Director at Apple for over a decade — running retail sales, service, and people operations across markets that mattered. I know what it looks like when an organization is humming and when it is quietly falling apart. I can usually tell the difference in the first fifteen minutes.

At GoPuff I was VP of Retail during a period of explosive growth and then real contraction. That experience taught me more about leadership than the growth years did. I have made hard decisions. I have been on the receiving end of them too.

I spent time as President of a private education company. I understand P&L, organizational culture, and what happens when a leadership team is performing for the board instead of building for the people.

I am not a consultant speaking from a distance. I have been in the room. I know what it costs.

Why Fortify exists
I built it because nothing else went far enough.

Traditional coaching respects the performance. It works with the professional, the output, the goal. It does not often go to the places that actually drive the performance — the body, the identity, the relationships, the parts of a leader's life that most frameworks will not touch.

I spent twenty years watching what that costs. Smart people, good people, high performers — quietly losing parts of themselves in exchange for the results. I did it too.

Fortify is built on 183 randomized controlled trials and the strongest evidence signal in wellbeing science: combined physical and psychological intervention produces nearly double the effect of either alone. That is not a philosophy. It is the research.

I am not a lost soul who decided to do coaching. I am someone who ran fast, delivered real things, paid real costs, and built a rigorous methodology because the gap was too important to fill with good intentions alone.

This is the work. I know what I am doing. And I know why it matters.
