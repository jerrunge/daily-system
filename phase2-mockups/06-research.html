The central premise
Every part of you affects every other part. Almost no framework is built on that premise.

When an executive is struggling — with performance, relationships, health, or identity — the problem rarely lives in a single domain. It lives in the cascade effects between them. Fortify™ maps that architecture: ten interconnected domains, three layers that organize them from biological prerequisites to professional expression, and the most consequential documented connections between them. Treating body and mind as separate coaching domains is not a philosophical disagreement. It is a category error with measurable consequences.

Combined intervention effect
Physical + psychological interventions together produce SMD = 0.73 versus approximately 0.45 for either alone. That gap is not incremental. It is the difference between coaching that works and coaching that feels good but doesn't move the needle.
Executive flourishing
Only 17.2% of adults are flourishing. 12.1% are languishing. The rest — 70% — are somewhere in between. High performance and deep struggle are not opposites. Keyes' two-continua model established this with MIDUS study data. Fortify is built on it.
Executive coaching efficacy
Psychologically-informed executive coaching shows a g = 0.59 effect size across 37 RCTs — a meaningful, replicable result. The operative word is psychologically-informed. Generic coaching shows much weaker effects.
Leadership contagion
Where executives model transparency about their own wellbeing, 72% of employees report above-average wellbeing. Where they don't: 57%. Leader wellbeing is not a private matter. It is an organizational variable.

26% of executives report depression symptoms vs. 18% in general workforce — often while fully functional. Keyes MIDUS: only 17.2% flourishing.

Brain & Focus
Chronic stress causes measurable prefrontal cortex dendritic atrophy after just 7 days. Largely reversible with recovery, exercise, and mindfulness. (Arnsten, Yale)
SX · Sexual
Intimacy & Desire
Absent from every major wellbeing framework. 46.79% of high-stress workers show sexual dysfunction. Partners show 71.8% difficulty rates vs. 38.1% in unaffected couples.
RL · Relational
Partnerships
Attachment insecurity correlates r = .42 with negative mental health (224 studies, 79,722 participants). Partner attachment style affects your cortisol reactivity and immune function.
SC · Social
Community
Loneliness carries equivalent mortality risk to smoking 15 cigarettes per day. 50% of CEOs report loneliness in role. Structured peer groups: 71% improved company performance.
FI · Financial
Security & Identity
For executives, compensation becomes identity. Adults with under $5K in assets have 2× the odds of depression. Financial stress drives 67% toward job changes regardless of other factors.
EN · Environmental
Space & Nature
120 minutes per week in nature produces measurable wellbeing improvement (White et al., 2019). Biophilic design improves cognitive performance 20% and job satisfaction 33%.
SP · Spiritual
Meaning & Purpose
VanderWeele's Global Flourishing Study (Harvard, 22 countries): spiritual engagement is among the strongest predictors of flourishing across cultures. Purpose in Life is the strongest predictor in Ryff's model.
ID · Identity
Self-Concept & Values
67% of C-suite executives "cover" core aspects of identity at work (Deloitte). 60% report emotional drain as a direct result. Identity change happens through action, not reflection alone. (Ibarra)

Four keystone connections
Identity Fusion · Financial ↔ Identity
Why executive job loss is psychologically violent
For executives, financial position and self-concept become so fused they cannot be cleanly separated. Job loss triggers identity crisis at a neurological level — not a professional setback, an existential one. Financial anxiety degrades self-concept. Fragile identity drives desperate financial decisions. This cascade runs in both directions and rarely gets named.
Hormonal Cascade · Physical ↔ Sexual
Chronic stress is not an excuse. It is a mechanism.
Sustained cortisol from overwork directly suppresses the HPG axis, reducing testosterone. One week of sleep restriction reduces testosterone up to 15%. The result is experienced as mysterious and shameful rather than understood as a physiological cascade. It is not a character flaw. It is a measurable consequence of how executives are asked to live.
Mind-Body Unity · Physical ↔ Mental
The strongest evidence signal we have
SMD = 0.73 for combined physical + psychological interventions versus approximately 0.45 for either alone. Exercise changes brain structure — reduces amygdala reactivity, increases BDNF, restores prefrontal function. Treating body and mind as separate coaching domains produces results that are real but half as strong as they could be.
Intimacy Spillover · Sexual ↔ Relational
Withdrawal reads as rejection, not stress
Partners of men with sexual dysfunction show 71.8% rates of sexual difficulty versus 38.1% in unaffected couples. Withdrawal that is the product of stress reads to partners as rejection. The relational damage deepens the original dysfunction and the isolation simultaneously. The cycle compounds unless the mechanism is named and addressed directly.
