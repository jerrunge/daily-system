Who you are
I have succeeded at hard things. The roles, the scale, the accomplishments — those were real. What I'm building now is the ground underneath them.
I am not failing at life. I am building the operating system I never had — one that lets me breathe, explore, be adventurous, laugh, and love without so much fear and angst.
The adventurous, laughing, loving version of me is not absent. He's just never had a nervous system regulated enough to come forward without fear running alongside him.
I identified my own self-sabotage pattern. That's not common. Most people never name it clearly enough to work with. I did — and I brought it to therapy.
How I show up
Connection is not a reward for finishing the to-do list. Jason, my partner, Maddy — these go on the calendar with the same weight as work.
I am not a substitute for human connection, but I am worthy of it. Leaning on my support network is not weakness — it's the thing I'm actively working to allow.
Maddy needs consistent contact points, not crisis response. Showing up predictably matters more than what I do in the moment.

Week A — Sunday prep guide
~2 hrs total. Put on a podcast, do it once, eat clean all week. Everything below is batched so daily meals are assembly only.
Proteins (60 min)
All three run simultaneously — start the chicken first, then eggs, then bison.
1
Chicken thighs (3 lbs) — coat in EVOO, garlic powder, paprika, salt. Roast at 400°F for 35 min. Cool, slice or pull apart, portion into containers.
2
Hard-boiled eggs (12) — cold water, bring to boil, 10 min, ice bath. Peel all, store in container with a damp paper towel.
3
Ground bison (2 lbs) — brown in skillet with cumin, garlic, salt. Break up fine. Drain any fat, cool, refrigerate in container.
4
Salmon (keep raw) — don't pre-cook. Takes 8 min fresh Tuesday and Friday. Way better than reheated.
Carbs (30 min)
1
White rice (3 cups dry) — cook per package. Yields ~6 cups cooked. Refrigerate in large container.
2
Sweet potatoes (4–5 medium) — pierce, microwave 5 min each or roast at 400°F for 45 min whole. Store whole in fridge — slice when needed.
Veg (25 min)
1
Sheet pan roasted veg — zucchini, peppers, broccoli in chunks. EVOO, salt, 425°F for 20–25 min. Roast until edges brown.
2
Wash and dry greens — spin dry, store in container lined with paper towel. Ready for salads all week.
3
Avocados — buy 3–4 at different ripeness stages. Don't prep — open fresh when needed. Your addition only (David allergic).
Seasoning setup (5 min)
Pull these out and leave them on the counter — reduces friction all week.
1
Coconut aminos, EVOO, and lemons on the counter.
2
Garlic powder, cumin, paprika, chipotle powder, Italian seasoning — line them up.
3
Dark chocolate in the freezer. Berries washed in the fridge.

1
Standing Hip Flexor Lunge
Anterior tilt · ED support · 45 sec each side
▾
Tight hip flexors are the #1 driver of anterior pelvic tilt and compress the pelvic floor. Releasing them daily targets your tilt, SI pressure, and blood flow through the pelvic region.
1
Step one foot forward into a lunge. Front knee over ankle, back leg straight.
2
Keep torso upright and hips square to the front.
3
Shift forward until you feel a stretch deep in the front of the back hip.
4
Hold 45 sec. Switch sides. 2 rounds each.
⏱ 90 sec total

2
Standing Figure-4 (Piriformis)
SI joint · ED support · 45 sec each side
▾
The piriformis sits directly next to the pudendal nerve — the main nerve involved in pelvic sensation. A tight piriformis = nerve compression. Also relieves SI joint tension.
1
Stand near a wall or fence. Cross one ankle over the opposite knee.
2
Slowly sit back and down until you feel a deep stretch in the glute of the crossed leg.
3
Keep spine long. Breathe into the stretch.
4
Hold 45 sec. Switch sides.
⏱ 90 sec total
☐  Mark done
3
Standing Pelvic Tilts
Anterior tilt · Low back reset · 60 sec
▾
Teaches your pelvis what neutral actually feels like — critical for someone who lifts heavy. Living in anterior tilt compresses the SI joint and shortens the pelvic floor chronically.
1
Feet hip-width, soft knees. Hands on hip bones.
2
Exaggerate your low back arch (your default anterior tilt).
3
Tuck tailbone under, squeeze glutes gently, flatten low back.
4
Rock slowly between the two, finding neutral. 15–20 reps.
⏱ ~60 sec
☐  Mark done
4
Standing Side Bend
Full spine · SI joint · 30 sec each side
▾
Opens the quadratus lumborum — the muscle that frequently pulls the SI joint out of alignment. Decompresses the spine after sleep.
1
Stand tall, feet hip-width. Raise the non-surgical arm straight overhead.
2
Lean slowly to the side until you feel a stretch along your whole side body.
3
Both feet planted, hips square. Don't twist.
4
Hold 30 sec each side. 2 rounds.
⚠ On the surgical side, keep that arm relaxed at your side. Only raise the non-surgical arm overhead.
⏱ 60 sec total

5
Cross-Body Shoulder Stretch
Posterior capsule · Rotator cuff · 30 sec × 3
▾
The posterior capsule is the most commonly tight structure after rotator cuff repair. This prevents compensatory patterns that stress the bicep tenodesis and cause impingement during lifting. Own this one forever.
1
Bring your surgical arm across your chest at shoulder height.
2
Use the other hand to support the elbow gently — don't pull, just hold.
3
Feel the stretch in the back of the shoulder. Keep the blade relaxed.
4
Hold 30 sec. Repeat 3×.
⚠ Gentle and consistent beats aggressive. No forcing, no pain.
⏱ 90 sec total
☐  Mark done
6
Diaphragmatic Breath + Pelvic Floor Release
ED support · Pelvic floor · Nervous system · ~2 min
▾
Weightlifters almost universally develop a hypertonic (chronically braced) pelvic floor. Deliberate release on the inhale — not Kegels — improves blood flow and nerve signaling. This is the most important move on the list for the ED symptoms. Pair with Pulsetto.
1
Stand or sit. One hand on belly, one on chest.
2
Inhale slowly through nose for 4–5 counts. Belly rises, chest stays still.
3
As you inhale, consciously let go of your pelvic floor — release downward.
4
Exhale slowly for 5–6 counts. 8–10 breaths total.
⏱ ~2 min
☐  Mark done
Start at Tier 1. Live there for 1–2 weeks before adding anything. The exhale is the master key.
Tier 1 — Foundation · Daily
Diaphragmatic Reset
5 minutes · Morning outdoors or before bed · Lying on your back, knees bent, feet flat. One hand on belly, one on chest.
Inhale through your nose for 4 counts — belly rises, chest stays still.

Pause at the top for 1 count — don't grip, just arrive.

Exhale through your nose for 6–8 counts — belly softens and falls.

On the inhale, invite your perineum to drop. Don't force it — just notice and allow. On the exhale, let it gently return to neutral. You're not doing a Kegel. You're removing the chronic grip.

Target: 10–15 breath cycles (~5 minutes).
Tier 2 — Vagus Nerve · Add after 1–2 weeks
Extended Exhale Breathing
Exhaling longer than you inhale is one of the most direct ways to activate the vagus nerve. Move through the progression as it feels comfortable.
Weeks 1–2: Inhale 4 · Exhale 6
Weeks 3–4: Inhale 4 · Exhale 7
Week 5+: Inhale 4 · Exhale 8

Don't rush to the 1:2 ratio. The nervous system needs time to trust it.

Position options: Lying down (best) or deep supported squat holding a doorframe — adds mechanical pelvic floor opening on top of the breathwork.
Tier 3 — Resonance · Add when Tier 2 feels natural
The 0.1 Hz Protocol
Slow-paced breathing at 0.1 Hz — approximately 6 breaths per minute — amplifies heart rate variability and significantly increases cardiac vagal activity. This is the breath rate used in clinical HRV training.
The breath: Inhale 5 counts · Exhale 5 counts · Exactly 6 breaths per minute.

Add pelvic floor coordination: Gently notice (don't grip) on the inhale — consciously release and drop on the exhale.

Duration: 10 minutes. This is the dose used in clinical research to produce measurable nervous system change.
Daily touchpoints
Perineum check-in (3× daily)
Set 3 random phone alarms labeled "drop." When it goes off: one slow breath, consciously release the perineum and pelvic floor. That's it. Over weeks this interrupts the automatic holding pattern.
Pre-lift reset
Before any heavy set, one full diaphragmatic breath — belly expands, pelvic floor drops on the inhale, then exhale and brace only as needed. Retrains the habitual over-brace pattern.
The bathroom practice
Feet elevated on a stool. Slow diaphragmatic breath in, and on the exhale consciously release the perineum downward. Do not strain. Let the breath do the work.
The hum (30 seconds daily)
Humming creates vibration in the throat that directly stimulates the vagus nerve. In the shower, in the car — it's genuine vagal toning. Do it daily.

Supine Spinal Twist
Low back · SI joint · 60 sec each side
▾
Decompresses the lumbar spine, releases the SI joint, and gently massages the digestive organs. One of the most effective end-of-day resets available — and completely passive.
1
Lie on your back. Draw one knee to your chest.
2
Gently guide that knee across your body toward the floor on the opposite side.
3
Extend the same-side arm out at shoulder height. Look away from the knee.
4
Hold 60 sec. Breathe deeply. Switch sides.
3
Child's Pose (Modified)
Full spine · Hip flexors · Low back · 90 sec
▾
Lengthens the entire posterior chain, decompresses the lumbar spine, and activates the parasympathetic nervous system through gentle forward folding. The modified version keeps the surgical shoulder safe.
1
From kneeling, sink your hips back toward your heels.
2
Walk hands forward until you feel a gentle stretch through the back. Keep arms relaxed — don't actively reach.
3
Rest your forehead on the floor or a pillow. Soften everything.
4
Hold 90 sec. Breathe deeply into your lower back.
⚠ Keep both arms at a comfortable distance — no overhead reaching with the surgical shoulder.
4
Legs Up the Wall
Nervous system reset · Circulation · 2–3 min
▾
This single position activates the parasympathetic nervous system, drains venous blood from the legs, reduces cortisol, and calms the racing-mind pattern that disrupts your sleep. The simplest and most powerful wind-down tool on this list.
1
Sit sideways against a wall, then swing your legs up as you lower your back to the floor.
2
Arms relaxed at your sides or hands on your belly. Close your eyes.
3
Breathe slowly and naturally. Let gravity do everything.
4
Stay 2–3 minutes or longer. Transition directly to your Tier 2 or 3 breathwork from here.

Rest day notes
Protein target stays the same on rest days — muscle protein synthesis peaks 24–48 hours after training. Carbs drop because you're not fueling acute work. Fat stays — testosterone production requires consistent fat intake, especially important given your current goals and medication stack.

Keep meals clean, keep protein high, reduce portion sizes slightly. Don't cut calories aggressively on rest days — it blunts recovery.
Daily non-negotiables
Protein every meal
Target 40–50g per meal across 4–5 meals. Spreading protein intake maximizes muscle protein synthesis throughout the day.
80g fats minimum
Critical for testosterone production. Don't go below 70g. Prioritize omega-3s, EVOO, avocado, eggs.

Why this matters
Skin is your largest organ and your first line of immune defense. At 40+, collagen production drops ~1% per year and UV damage compounds. A 5-minute daily routine with the right actives is the highest-ROI investment in how you look and how your skin ages. You already have most of what you need.
Morning — 2 to 3 minutes
Cleanse, defend, protect — and lock in body moisture while the shower's still on your skin. Morning is about shielding from the day's oxidative assault. Vitamin C neutralizes free radicals before they do damage. SPF is non-negotiable. Aveeno goes on last while still damp.

Night — 2 to 3 minutes
Cleanse, repair, restore. Night is when skin goes into repair mode — cell turnover peaks between 11pm and 4am. Niacinamide calms and strengthens. Retinol accelerates the cell renewal that naturally slows with age. This is where the real work happens.
