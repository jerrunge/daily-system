Standing Hip Flexor Lunge
Anterior tilt · ED support · 45 sec each side
▾
Tight hip flexors are the #1 driver of anterior pelvic tilt. Releasing them daily targets your tilt, SI pressure, and blood flow through the pelvic region.
1
Step one foot forward into a lunge. Front knee over ankle, back leg straight.
2
Keep torso upright and hips square to the front.
3
Shift forward until you feel a stretch deep in the front of the back hip.
4
Hold 45 sec. Switch sides. 2 rounds each.
⏱ 90 sec total

2
Standing Figure-4 (Piriformis)
SI joint · ED support · 45 sec each side
▾
The piriformis sits directly next to the pudendal nerve. A tight piriformis = nerve compression. Also relieves SI joint tension.
1
Stand near a wall. Cross one ankle over the opposite knee.
2
Slowly sit back and down until you feel a deep stretch in the crossed-leg glute.
3
Keep spine long. Breathe into the stretch.
4
Hold 45 sec. Switch sides.
⏱ 90 sec total
☐ Mark done

Standing Pelvic Tilts
Anterior tilt · Low back reset · 60 sec
▾
Teaches your pelvis what neutral actually feels like — critical for someone who lifts heavy.
1
Feet hip-width, soft knees. Hands on hip bones.
2
Exaggerate your low back arch (your default anterior tilt).
3
Tuck tailbone under, squeeze glutes gently, flatten low back.
4
Rock slowly between the two, finding neutral. 15–20 reps.
⏱ ~60 sec

Standing Side Bend
Full spine · SI joint · QL · 30 sec each side
▾
Opens the quadratus lumborum — the muscle that frequently pulls the SI joint out of alignment.
1
Stand tall, one arm straight overhead, palm facing in.
2
Lean slowly to the opposite side, reaching the overhead arm long.
3
Both feet planted, hips square. Don't rotate forward.
4
Hold 30 sec each side. 2 rounds each.
⏱ 120 sec total
☐ Mark done

Cross-Body Posterior Shoulder Stretch
Posterior capsule · Rotator cuff · 30 sec × 3 each side
▾
The posterior shoulder capsule is one of the most commonly tight structures in lifters. Tightness causes impingement and compensatory forward head posture.
1
Bring one arm across your chest at shoulder height.
2
Use the opposite hand to support the elbow — gentle inward pressure.
3
Feel the stretch in the back of the shoulder. Keep shoulder blade down.
4
Hold 30 sec. Switch sides. 3 rounds each.
⏱ 3 min total

Diaphragmatic Breath + Pelvic Floor Release
ED support · Pelvic floor · Nervous system · ~2 min
▾
Weightlifters almost universally develop a hypertonic pelvic floor. Deliberate release on the inhale — not Kegels — improves blood flow and nerve signaling. Pair with Pulsetto.
1
Stand or sit. One hand on belly, one on chest.
2
Inhale slowly through nose for 4–5 counts. Belly rises, chest stays still.
3
As you inhale, consciously let go of your pelvic floor — release downward.
4
Exhale slowly for 5–6 counts. 8–10 breaths total.
⏱ ~2 min
☐ Mark done
7
Doorway Chest Opener
Pec minor/major · Anterior shoulder · 30 sec × 3
▾
Heavy pressing chronically shortens the pec minor, tilting the scapula forward — the root cause of most lifter shoulder impingement.
1
Stand in a doorframe. Forearms on the frame, elbows at 90°.
2
Step one foot through and shift weight forward until you feel a stretch across the chest.
3
Core lightly engaged. Stretch is in the chest, not the lumbar spine.
4
Hold 30 sec. Rest 10 sec. Repeat 3 rounds.
⏱ ~2 min
☐ Mark done

Standing Thoracic Extension
Upper back · T-spine mobility · Overhead path · 60 sec
▾
Thoracic kyphosis is the single biggest limiter of overhead pressing range and a major driver of shoulder impingement.
1
Feet hip-width. Interlace hands behind head, elbows wide.
2
Gently draw elbows back, open chest toward ceiling. Mid-back is the hinge.
3
Hold 2–3 sec, breathe into the stretch. Return to neutral. 10–12 reps.
4
Keep low back relaxed — thoracic extension, not lumbar.
⏱ ~60 sec

Tier 1 — Foundation · Daily
Diaphragmatic Reset
5 min · Morning outdoors or before bed · On your back, knees bent, feet flat. One hand on belly, one on chest.
Inhale through nose for 4 counts — belly rises, chest stays still.

Pause 1 count.

Exhale through nose for 6–8 counts — belly softens.

On the inhale, invite your perineum to drop. Don't force it — just allow. You're removing the chronic grip.

Target: 10–15 breath cycles (~5 min).
Tier 2 — Vagus Nerve · Add after 1–2 weeks
Extended Exhale Breathing
Exhaling longer than you inhale directly activates the vagus nerve.
Weeks 1–2: Inhale 4 · Exhale 6
Weeks 3–4: Inhale 4 · Exhale 7
Week 5+: Inhale 4 · Exhale 8

Don't rush to the 1:2 ratio. The nervous system needs time to trust it.
Tier 3 — Resonance · Add when Tier 2 feels natural
The 0.1 Hz Protocol
~6 breaths per minute — amplifies HRV and cardiac vagal activity. The dose used in clinical research.
The breath: Inhale 5 counts · Exhale 5 counts · Exactly 6 breaths per minute.

Add pelvic floor coordination: gently notice on the inhale, consciously release on the exhale.

Duration: 10 minutes.
Daily touchpoints
Perineum check-in (3× daily)
Set 3 random alarms labeled "drop." When it fires: one slow breath, consciously release. That's it. Over weeks this interrupts the automatic holding pattern.
Pre-lift reset
Before any heavy set, one full diaphragmatic breath — belly expands, pelvic floor drops on the inhale, then exhale and brace only as needed.
The hum (30 sec daily)
Humming creates vibration that directly stimulates the vagus nerve. In the shower, in the car — genuine vagal toning. Do it daily.

SI joint · Pelvic floor · 60 sec each side
▾
Gravity-assisted and deeper than the morning version. Excellent for resetting SI joint tension that built up through the day and gym.
1
Lie on your back, knees bent. Cross one ankle over opposite knee.
2
Either stay here or gently draw both legs toward your chest.
3

4

2
Supine Spinal Twist
Low back · SI joint · 60 sec each side
▾
Decompresses the lumbar spine, releases the SI joint, and gently massages the digestive organs.
1
Lie on your back. Draw one knee to your chest.
2
Guide that knee across your body toward the floor on the opposite side.
3
Extend the same-side arm out at shoulder height. Look away from the knee.
4
Hold 60 sec. Switch sides.
3
Child's Pose (Modified)
Full spine · Hip flexors · 90 sec
▾
Decompresses the lumbar spine and activates the parasympathetic nervous system. Modified version keeps the surgical shoulder safe.
1
From kneeling, sink hips back toward heels.
2
Walk hands forward until you feel a gentle stretch. Keep arms relaxed.
3
Rest forehead on floor or pillow. Soften everything.
4
Hold 90 sec. Breathe deeply into your lower back.
⚠ Keep both arms at comfortable distance — no overhead reaching with the surgical shoulder.
4
Legs Up the Wall
Nervous system reset · Circulation · 2–3 min
▾
Activates the parasympathetic nervous system, drains venous blood from the legs, reduces cortisol. The simplest and most powerful wind-down tool on this list.
1
Sit sideways against a wall, swing legs up as you lower your back to the floor.
2
Arms relaxed at sides or on belly. Close your eyes.
3
Breathe slowly. Let gravity do everything.
4
Stay 2–3 minutes. Transition directly to Tier 2 or 3 breathwork from here.

3
Ground bison (2 lbs) — brown with cumin, garlic, salt. Drain, cool, refrigerate.
4
Salmon (keep raw) — don't pre-cook. 8 min fresh is way better.

1

2

3

4
Wash and dry greens — spin dry, store with paper towel. Ready all week.
