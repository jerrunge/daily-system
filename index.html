Tier 1 -- Foundation - Daily
Diaphragmatic Reset
5 minutes - Morning outdoors or before bed - Lying on your back, knees bent, feet flat.
Inhale through your nose for 4 counts -- belly rises, chest stays still.

Pause at the top for 1 count.

Exhale through your nose for 6-8 counts -- belly softens.

On the inhale, invite your perineum to drop. On the exhale, let it gently return to neutral. You're not doing a Kegel. You're removing the chronic grip.

Target: 10-15 breath cycles (~5 minutes).
Tier 2 -- Vagus Nerve - Add after 1-2 weeks
Extended Exhale Breathing
Exhaling longer than you inhale is one of the most direct ways to activate the vagus nerve.
Weeks 1-2: Inhale 4 - Exhale 6
Weeks 3-4: Inhale 4 - Exhale 7
Week 5+: Inhale 4 - Exhale 8

Don't rush to the 1:2 ratio. The nervous system needs time to trust it.
Tier 3 -- Resonance - Add when Tier 2 feels natural
The 0.1 Hz Protocol
~6 breaths per minute. Amplifies heart rate variability. Used in clinical HRV training.
The breath: Inhale 5 counts - Exhale 5 counts - 6 breaths per minute.

Add pelvic floor coordination: Notice on the inhale -- consciously release and drop on the exhale.

Duration: 10 minutes.
Daily touchpoints
Perineum check-in (3x daily)
Set 3 random phone alarms labeled "drop." When it goes off: one slow breath, consciously release the perineum.
Pre-lift reset
Before any heavy set, one full diaphragmatic breath -- belly expands, pelvic floor drops on the inhale, then exhale and brace only as needed.
The bathroom practice
Feet elevated on a stool. Slow breath in, and on the exhale consciously release the perineum downward. Do not strain.
The hum (30 seconds daily)
Humming creates vibration in the throat that directly stimulates the vagus nerve. In the shower, in the car. Do it daily.

Main compounds go heavy (6-8 reps near failure). Accessories stay 10-15. Double progression on accessories â add reps first, then weight. On compounds â add weight when all 4 sets hit the top of the range clean.
The rule
Double Progression
Week 1: Incline DB Press 4 x 10 @ 50 lbs. Keep 50 lbs until you can do 4 x 12 with good form. Then move to 55 lbs and start back at 4 x 10.
Minimum effective dose
Log every set in Hevy. Every session. Not tracking is not knowing.
16-Week Phases
Weeks 1-4 -- Build the Foundation
Rep ranges: 10-15 reps, controlled form, no ego weight. Master the movement patterns and establish baseline numbers. Deload Week 4: Drop to 2-3 sets at 50-60% weight.
Weeks 5-8 -- Intensify
Rest-pause sets: On last set of compound movements, hit target reps, rest 15 sec, squeeze out 3-5 more. Slow eccentrics: 4-second lowering phase on all pressing movements.
Weeks 9-12 -- Peak Volume
Add one extra set to main compound movements (5 sets instead of 4). Supersets on non-competing muscle groups. Add 2 longer Cooper walks per week -- 45+ min, include hills.
Weeks 13-16 -- Strength + Burn
Main compounds move to 6-8 reps at heavier weight. Accessory work stays 12-15. Final deload Week 16. Assess progress, take measurements, photo, reset.
Deload Protocol
Deload weeks: 4, 8, 12, 16. Drop to 2-3 sets at 50-60% weight. Keep calories identical. Skipping deloads leads to plateaus and injury.
Tracking
Weekly weigh-in
Weigh every morning after bathroom. Average the 7 days on Sunday. The 7-day average is the truth.
Every 2 weeks
Progress photo -- same spot, same light, same time. Tape measurements: waist, hips, chest.
Red flags -- see your doctor
Weight stalls 3+ weeks: thyroid check. Unusual fatigue: hematocrit check (TRT). Joint pain not resolving after deload: reduce volume. BP consistently above 140/90: don't wait.
