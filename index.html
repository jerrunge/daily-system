The core problem
Ad hoc eating with clean-food standards means every meal requires a decision AND sourcing AND prep. Fix: batch once on Sunday, assemble the rest of the week.
Sunday batch — 2 to 2.5 hrs
Proteins — cook all at once
Kirkland organic chicken thighs (bulk-cook Sunday, portion and freeze) · Ground bison patties (Costco, pre-formed, fridge or freezer) · Kirkland wild-caught Alaskan salmon (sockeye/coho, frozen) · Vital Farms pasture-raised eggs (dozen hard-boiled)
Carbs + veg — roast together
Sweet potatoes or organic white rice · Sheet pan roasted veg (zucchini, broccoli, peppers) · Washed greens for quick salads
Fats — shelf-stable
Avocados staggered to ripen across the week · Organic EVOO, raw nuts, coconut aminos ready to go
Daily assembly — not cooking
Post-workout
Grab pre-cooked chicken, salmon, or beef — scoop rice or sweet potato. Done in 5 min. Within 45 min of gym. Wild Planet tuna is the no-prep backup.
Midday
Big salad + batch protein + avocado + EVOO + lemon. No cooking. Works for Maddy too.
Dinner
One real cook of the day. Simple protein + veg. The community meal — partner or Maddy involved. Shared cooking is connection.
The mess problem
Batch day = one mess, cleaned once. Daily assembly = bowls and a fork. Same total work, restructured so it doesn't scatter across the week.
Costco — monthly anchor
Wild salmon · organic chicken thighs · ground bison · pasture-raised eggs · organic sweet potatoes · organic frozen veg · raw nuts · coconut aminos · organic EVOO · avocados. Calendar event, not reactive errand.
Why this matters
Skin is your largest organ and your first line of immune defense. At 40+, collagen production drops ~1% per year and UV damage compounds. A 5-minute daily routine with the right actives is the highest-ROI investment in how you look and how your skin ages. You already have most of what you need.
Morning — 2 to 3 minutes
Cleanse, defend, protect — and lock in body moisture while the shower's still on your skin. Morning is about shielding from the day's oxidative assault. Vitamin C neutralizes free radicals before they do damage. SPF is non-negotiable. Aveeno goes on last while still damp.
1
Cetaphil Cleanser
Gentle, pH-balanced. Removes overnight sebum without stripping your moisture barrier. You already have it — use it.
Already owned
2
Vitamin C Serum
Apply, let absorb 60 seconds. Vitamin C (L-ascorbic acid) is the most evidence-backed antioxidant in skincare. Neutralizes free radicals, stimulates collagen synthesis, fades hyperpigmentation. Most effective in the morning when UV exposure is ahead of you.
60 sec absorption
3
Brickell Daily Defense SPF 30
Clean formula, SPF 30 mineral protection. UV is responsible for ~80% of visible facial aging. This is the single most important anti-aging step in any routine. Every day — including Pacifica's overcast days. UV penetrates cloud cover.
Non-negotiable daily
4
Aveeno Body Lotion
Apply while skin is still damp — within 3 minutes of the shower. Moisture absorption is dramatically higher when the skin barrier is open and hydrated. Oat-based, proven to restore the skin barrier. At your height there's a lot of surface area to cover — this keeps it healthy, not just comfortable. You already have it.
Already owned · damp skin only
Night — 2 to 3 minutes
Cleanse, repair, restore. Night is when skin goes into repair mode — cell turnover peaks between 11pm and 4am. Niacinamide calms and strengthens. Retinol accelerates the cell renewal that naturally slows with age. This is where the real work happens.
1
Cetaphil Cleanser
Remove the day — sunscreen, sebum, environmental particulates. Clean skin absorbs actives far more effectively. Don't skip this.
Already owned
2
Niacinamide Serum
Apply, let absorb 60 seconds. Vitamin B3. Strengthens the skin barrier, reduces pore appearance, controls oil, fades dark spots, and — critically — reduces the irritation that retinol can cause when layered underneath it. Apply before retinol every night.
60 sec absorption
3
CeraVe Resurfacing Retinol Serum
Retinol is the gold standard of anti-aging actives — decades of clinical evidence. Accelerates cell turnover, stimulates collagen, reduces fine lines and discoloration. The niacinamide underneath buffers irritation. Give it 4–6 weeks before judging results.
The heavy lifter
4
Cetaphil Lotion
Lock in moisture after the retinol. Seals the skin barrier, reduces any dryness or flaking. You already have it — apply as the final step to keep retinol's benefits without the tightness.
Already owned
The stack in context
This routine costs 5–6 minutes total across morning and night. The actives — Vitamin C, niacinamide, retinol, SPF — are the four most clinically validated ingredients in modern dermatology. You're not doing a luxury routine. You're doing a science-backed one with what works.
