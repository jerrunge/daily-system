The frame

One unfair advantage drives all of this: the intimacy is not performed. The category is faceless apps doing a warm voice behind a logo. You have a real dad and a real daughter who built this together. Every move below is a way to let that truth do the work a logo cannot.

What winning looks like (and what it is not): not view counts. It is qualified follows from caregivers and AuDHD adults, practitioner relationships that refer, warm waitlist signups, and Maddy's comfort staying intact. A 1,200-view video that opens one OT conversation beats a 40,000-view video that converts nobody.

The spine

Jeremy the dad, telling the truth, in short video. Carries the brand with or without Maddy. Start here.

The moat

Practitioners who vouch. No audience or budget needed. Highest leverage per hour. One OT refers across AuDHD, ADHD, and TBI.

The multiplier

Maddy's voice at the rung she chooses, and the two of you together, used rarely and powerfully.

Three things never move

The honesty boundary: your story goes anywhere; "Maddy is proven to help X" never does. Name the mechanism or the research as the subject of any cross-population claim, never Maddy. Maddy's consent and comfort: per piece, revocable, hers. The in-product voice: inside the app, it stays built-for-the-user. Founder-forward is the trust wrapper, not a change to the product's voice.

01

The consent operating system

Because Maddy is in this, the system for how she is in it comes before the creative. She is 21, so this is her decision to make and unmake. The child-influencer laws do not apply. What applies is care, by choice: the thing that makes her involvement powerful is that it is real, and real breaks the instant she is pushed. Run her participation the way the app runs its Circle: opt-in, granular, centered on her, revocable.

Her lead
She drives it. Her feature, her story, her voice as the AuDHD adult it was built with. At 21, fully hers to choose, held only to comfort and the guardrails.

The per-piece flow

  1. Propose. You name the piece, the surface, and the rung you are asking for. One sentence each.
  2. She reviews the finished asset, not a description. Approving an idea and approving the real thing are different.
  3. Cooling beat. Anything she is unsure about does not ship that day. She sleeps on it.
  4. Two yeses. Hers, and yours as the person protecting her and the brand. Her no always wins. Your safety concern pauses for a conversation, it does not override an adult.
  5. Publish, then log in JER-384 with the rung and her explicit yes.
  6. Revocable. On owned surfaces you can pull it anytime. For a creator reshare, you tell her the honest limit up front.

Guardrails that run in the background

  • Comment shield. Default: she does not read comments. You or a filter triage. Harassment gets hidden, blocked, reported. She sees the warm ones only if she wants to.
  • Permanence lens. Content is forever. "Would future her be glad this exists." At 21 that is hers to weigh, with you.
  • Masking watch. If a piece makes her perform a self instead of being herself, it is the wrong piece, however well it would do.
  • Comfort is the metric. If her comfort ever competes with a number, comfort wins. A coerced moment reads as inauthentic, and inauthentic torches the whole advantage. The right call and the winning call are the same call.

Her optional own presence

If she ever wants a creator presence of her own, an AuDHD adult talking to other AuDHD adults as one of them, that is available and it is hers, not a deliverable you assign. Supported if she wants it, never required.

The shooting system

  • Gear: your phone, natural light from a window, a cheap tripod. A clip mic is nice, not required. Stop here. Production value is not the currency, honesty and specificity are.
  • Length: 30 to 75 seconds. Shorter is usually better.
  • Structure every time: hook (3s) → the story or problem (15 to 40s) → the turn or insight → a soft call. One idea per video.
  • Batch: film 6 to 8 in one 90-minute sitting, once or twice a month. This is the single thing that prevents burnout. You are not filming daily.
  • Captions: always, burned in. Most people watch muted.
  • B-roll to grab once: the app screen, hands, a sink of dishes, a door, a phone timer, walking, morning light. Five minutes of cutaways covers a month.
  • Editing: CapCut or similar, minimal. The "good enough" bar is clear audio, captions, one hook. Ship at good enough. Polish is procrastination here.

04

Track: Together the heart

The dad-and-daughter co-design conversation is the single most powerful asset available. Precisely because it is so strong, use it sparingly. Scarcity keeps it real and protects her.

Forms

  • "She asked for this feature. Here is the conversation we had." (Her at her chosen rung, can be her words over the story.)
  • Designing a small thing together, shown honestly.
  • Her reacting to something you built from her feedback.

The scarcity rule is the strategy, not timidity

A handful of together-pieces a year reads as genuine collaboration. A weekly together-series reads as mining the relationship for content, which is inauthentic no matter how real it is, and it would quietly torch the trust advantage the whole plan is built on. Rare and powerful beats frequent and cheap.

The practitioner moat highest leverage

Your single best door, and it needs no audience and no ad budget. Caregivers trust practitioners more than any ad, because that person knows their kid. One occupational therapist who likes Maddy refers a stream of pre-trusted, high-intent caregivers. And because OTs do executive-function work across populations, one relationship refers AuDHD, ADHD, and TBI clients at once. The moat and the "+" compound.

Segments (in priority order)

  4. State developmental-disability service coordinators. Warm structural start via SARC (JER-45/46). Slower, durable, gatekeeper economics.

The outreach sequence (per practitioner)

  1. Touch 0, warm up. Follow, and genuinely engage with their content for a week. No cold approach.
  2. Touch 1, the founder note. Short. A dad who built this with his AuDHD daughter, asking for their eyes. Not a pitch. Offer the one-pager and a free look.
  3. Touch 2, one follow-up after about a week if no reply, value-add (the research base or a single relevant link), then stop. No third chase.
  4. If interested: send the one-pager plus a TestFlight or demo, and offer a 15-minute call.
  5. The reviewer is the referrer. The OT who reads it pre-publish is your first referrer. This is how the open OT-reviewer gate (JER-471) and the caregiver-reach problem get solved by the same relationship.

07

Lives depth, later

Lives deepen an audience that already exists. They do almost nothing to create one. A live to an empty room is demoralizing and a waste of your scarcest resource. The order matters: short-form gathers people first, lives deepen them after.

  • Threshold: start when you have roughly 1 to 2K engaged followers, or a warm email list that will reliably show up. Not before.
  • Formats: "ask a dad who built this," office hours, a co-design Q and A, a build-in-public update. Occasionally with Maddy, her choice, per Section 01.
  • Cadence: monthly to start. Announce a day ahead. One topic, three talking points, open questions, a soft call at the end.
08

Word of mouth and the site fix

Parents are already recommending Maddy by hand. That is the rarest, most valuable signal you have, and the cheapest growth in the plan. Two moves.

Make sharing frictionless

  • A "bring another parent in" share link, clean and caregiver-shaped.
  • A gentle referral prompt inside the beta-invite email and at a moment of delight in the app.
  • A shareable caregiver-shaped card (not a user-shaped one) for the parents already advocating.

Fix what a warm caregiver lands on

Right now mymaddy.app may speak mainly to the AuDHDer who would use it ("starting is the wall"). A caregiver arriving from word of mouth needs to see themselves immediately, or they bounce, and you leak the exact interest that is converting. Add a caregiver path: "You are a parent or a supporter? Here is how Maddy helps, and how you join their Circle," with a clear waitlist CTA for caregivers. This is a Webflow change I can make.

12

How this fails (and the guard against each)

Chasing a viral hit

There is no single video that saves this. Guard: judge by the dashboard, not by any one post. Compounding or nothing.

Worshipping views

The failure you are living now. Guard: the only numbers on the wall are saves, signups, and practitioner replies.

Founder burnout

Daily for three weeks, then resentment, then silence. Guard: batch monthly, 2 to 3 posts a week, never daily.

Maddy at the center

Her face converts, which is the temptation. Guard: restraint by design; she leads only when she chooses, never as the default engine.

Lives too early

Going live to nobody and concluding video does not work. Guard: the audience threshold before the first live.

Going faceless when it is slow

The first quiet month, the pull is to hide behind the logo. Guard: the face is the strategy; the slow month is the cost of the only durable asset.

Performed authenticity

This community punishes masking and ad-cosplay instantly. Guard: lead with real life, own the failures, never script a feeling.

Outreach that smells like sales

Practitioners and creators reject a pitch. Guard: it is an invitation and an honest ask for a read, never a close.

Channel sprawl

Spreading thin across six platforms. Guard: TikTok and IG first; everything else is repurpose or later.
