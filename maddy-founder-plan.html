▸The frame: the bet, the audience, what winning means

One unfair advantage drives everything here: the intimacy is not performed. The category is faceless apps doing a warm voice behind a logo. Maddy is a real dad and a real daughter who built this together. Every move below lets that truth do the work a logo cannot.

What winning looks like (and what it is not)

Not view counts. The scoreboard is qualified follows from caregivers and AuDHD adults, practitioner relationships that refer, warm waitlist signups, and Maddy's comfort staying intact. A 1,200-view video that opens one OT conversation beats a 40,000-view video that converts nobody. If you ever feel the pull to optimize for reach, that is the plan failing, not working.

The spine

Jeremy the dad, telling the truth, in short video. Carries the brand with or without Maddy. Start here.

The moat

Practitioners who vouch. No audience or budget needed. The most return per hour. One OT refers across AuDHD, ADHD, and the wider executive-function world.

The multiplier

Maddy's voice at the rung she chooses, and the two of you together, used rarely and powerfully.

The audience, locked

AuDHD is the anchor and the origin: built with an AuDHD co-designer, for AuDHD. The door is wider than the anchor. Maddy is for any brain where starting is the wall: ADHD, autism, TBI and stroke, long COVID, perimenopause, depression, burnout. Canonical line: "AuDHD+ designed to support all executive-functioning needs." The breadth lives in the message, the keywords, and who you talk to. It never lives in the promise. That distinction is the next box.

Three things never move

The honesty boundary. Your story goes anywhere. "Maddy is proven to help X" goes nowhere. Name the mechanism or the research as the subject of any cross-population claim, never Maddy. This is what keeps the wide door safe and the founder credible. It is also what an OT checks before they refer you.

Maddy's consent and comfort. Per piece, revocable, hers. Founder-forward means more of Jeremy's story, not more of her private experience.

The in-product voice. Inside the app it stays built-for-the-user. Founder-forward is the trust wrapper around the product, not a change to the product's voice.
