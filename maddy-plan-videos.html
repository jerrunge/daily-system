The video build guide

This is the one place the video work lives. Each card is one video, shoot-and-hand-back ready: it opens with a hook built to stop the scroll, speaks to one of the four acquisition audiences (the Circle roles), shows how Maddy answers what that person is actually struggling with, and ends with the real ask, the free beta. Below the four audiences sit Maddy's own co-design videos, in her voice, filmed only when she opts in. Film it, drop the clip in iCloud, I finish it in Descript, you post.

☁
Handoff: drop the raw clip in iCloud Drive, full resolution, no re-export. I pull it straight into Descript. Size is not a factor. The b-roll and app screens each card needs are already in the vault, linked on the card.

The production standard
The eight, every video

These hold across every card so the feed reads as one voice. The per-card Descript brief tells you what's specific; this is what's always true. Tagged by who does it.

  1. Say it first. (you, on camera) Your opening words are the hook. No "so," no "today I want to talk about," no "hey everybody." The hardest, truest line comes out first.
  2. Hook card. (me, in edit) Big on-screen text for the first ~1.5 seconds, upper third above your face, then it clears. This is what stops a muted scroll.
  3. Punch-ins. (me, in edit) A subtle zoom on two or three emphasis beats to carry energy and hide cuts. You don't film these.
  4. Tighten. (you + me) Target 30 to 40 seconds. Every gap and breath gets cut. Energy over completeness.
  5. Captions. (me, in edit) All-caps, white with a heavy shadow, yellow active word, sitting at 82% down the frame so nothing clips on mobile. Burned in, so they travel with the file.
  6. Button. (you, on camera) End on the line that resonates, never on "more to come" or "anyway." The card gives you the closing line.
  7. Audio. (you, after) The master is voice plus captions, no music. On TikTok you add a trending sound in-app at 15 to 20 percent, original audio at 100.
  8. One idea, vertical. (you, on camera) 9:16, one idea per video. If it's two ideas, it's two videos.

Recurring co-design formats (future, when the moment is real, and only if she opts in): the feature test (the two of you trying something in the app, honest reactions, including when it doesn't work), the interview (her leading), the origin told together (one piece, used sparingly), and the milestone (launch day or a big review, marked together).

Two guardrails that never move: the honesty boundary (mechanism-level only pre-launch, the subject of any cross-population claim is the research or the design, never "Maddy fixes X"), and the founding price stays out of video copy until the two-product setup ships.

How this board works
  1. Shoot from the card. The hook block is the open, the beats are the spine, the ask is the close. One idea per video.
  2. Drop the clip in iCloud Drive. Full resolution, no re-export. I pull it into Descript and run the brief on the card.
  3. I hand it back as a published review link: hook built, b-roll cut in, captions burned, audio cleaned.
  4. Close it out by tapping the status: Planned, Filming, Editing, Posted. Saves on this device, and the board reads the same state on your other devices.
  5. Consent-gated cards (the ones telling Maddy's story) ask you to confirm the JER-384 entry is logged before posting. The dashboard counts what's still open.
  6. Keep me in sync: tap "Copy status for the vault" and paste it back, and I log it so the board and I always agree. Say "deploy the backend" and edits sync across all three devices on their own.
