Creators

ND creators have already built the trust you are trying to earn. The right collaboration borrows it. The wrong one reads as an ad and burns it. Lead with the human story, never with "promote my app."

Tiers and what to ask for

TierExamples (sized)Realistic ask
Micro (10K to 150K)ND-affirming OTs and coaches; smaller AuDHD creatorsGenuine relationship, a duet or a "founder I met" feature, an honest first look. Start here.
Mid (150K to 700K)Established AuDHD and ADHD creatorsA collab once you have social proof; co-made content, not a read-out.
Mega (700K+)The biggest ND voicesLater, warm intro only, when the story is strong enough to be interesting to them. Not a cold transactional pitch.

What to ask for (in order of trust)

  • A duet or stitch of one of your origin or recognition videos. Lowest lift, highest authenticity.
  • An honest first look, told in their own voice, including critique. Real beats glowing.
  • A co-made piece about the problem (task initiation, the shame spiral), with the app as one part, not the point.
  • Her peer presence, only if Maddy wants it: an AuDHD adult talking with an AuDHD creator, peer to peer.
Creator outreach DM
Hi [name], your video on [specific thing] said something I have lived from the other side, as a dad. My daughter is AuDHD, and the two of us built an app together after everything we tried failed her. It is called Maddy, and it is built around the stuff you talk about: task initiation, the shame spiral, no breakable streaks, no failure states. I am not looking for an ad. I would love to just share the story with you, and if any of it resonates, maybe we make something honest together, critique included. If not, I will still be here watching your work. Thank you for it. Jeremy Maddy's dad jeremy@mymaddy.app

Do not

Do not send a generic "love your content, want to promote my app" DM. Do not ask for a scripted endorsement. Do not offer payment as the opener. Voice contamination is a real risk: their voice and the Maddy voice must both stay intact, which means co-made, not bought.

Going live

Lives convert the people who already half-trust you into people who fully do. They are real-time, unedited, and human, which is exactly the founder-forward advantage at full strength. Start once there is a small audience to show up.

Threshold

Wait until roughly 500 to 1,000 engaged followers, enough that a handful reliably show up live. Before that, put the energy into the video bank and the practitioner moat.

Formats, with a run-of-show

  • Build-in-public office hours (monthly). 20 to 30 min. Open with what you shipped, show one thing on screen, take questions. Run-of-show: 2 min welcome, 8 min update and demo, 15 min Q and A, 2 min where to follow.
  • Ask-a-dad-who-built-this. Caregiver questions, answered honestly. You are not a clinician and you say so; you share what you learned as a parent. Run-of-show: 2 min frame and disclaimer, 20 min questions, 3 min resources.
  • With a practitioner or creator. Once relationships exist (Sections 05 and 06): a conversation, not an interview. The guest's audience meets you in a trusted context.
  • Maddy co-host, only if she wants it. Peer-to-peer with the community. Highest trust, entirely her call, comment-shield pre-set.

Promote and repurpose

  • Announce 24 hours ahead in a story and a post. Remind 1 hour before.
  • Record every live. The best 2 to 3 minutes become short clips. One live can feed a week of short-form.
  • The full recording becomes a YouTube upload (now in scope), searchable and evergreen.

Word of mouth + the site

Word of mouth is the only channel that has converted for you so far, which is a signal, not a limit. The job is to give it more surface area and a destination that does not drop the trust the video built.

Fuel word of mouth

  • Make the shareable thing the recognition, not the ad. Ep 05, 09, 11 get shared because they name an experience. Every share carries the brand without selling.
  • Give caregivers language to pass on. "Scaffolding without nagging," "the start is the wall," "support designed to fade." Phrases travel further than features.
  • Ask warmly, once trust exists. "If this helped you see your kid differently, send it to one parent who needs it."
