01 Caregiver She could fly across the world alone. She could not start the dishes. posted ▸
⚡The hook (first 2 seconds)
First frame
Hard split screen. Left: a departures board or world map. Right: a sink stacked with dishes. No face yet.
The draw
The gap is the visual. Cut hard between the two halves on the first beat, then collapse them into one frame. The eye finishes the contradiction before you speak.
The line
"She could fly across the world alone. She couldn't start the dishes."
For
Caregiver. The parent or partner who has watched this exact gap and been told it was defiance. Naming it as task initiation is a relief, and relief gets saved and shared.
Beats
0-3sthe contradiction. 3-15sthe two side by side: the big thing she can do, the small thing that stops her. 15-35sthe insight: novel tasks come with structure, urgency, a destination. Daily tasks come with none. 35-50sname it: initiation, not laziness.
Payoff
"Her brain can do the hard thing. It just cannot generate the start on its own. So we built the start."
On screen
capability is not the probleminitiation isthis is not laziness
Call
Follow. No link on this one. Let it land.
Why it works
The contradiction sounds impossible, stops the scroll, then resolves into recognition. That turn from "wait, what" to "that is my kid" is the whole engine.
Maddy
Named only is plenty. Her words optional: one line she wrote about the gap.

05 User It is not the doing. It is the starting. planned ▸
⚡The hook (first 2 seconds)
First frame
A single unstarted thing, held still: one dish in an empty sink, or a blinking cursor on an empty list. Dead quiet.
The draw
Everything freezes but one pulse. The whole frame goes still, then one element breathes (the cursor, the tap target). The stillness is the feeling of being stuck. Then a sharp text snap on the word "starting."
The line
"It's not the doing. It's the starting."
For
User. The AuDHDer hearing their own experience named without a fix attached. Not "here is how to be productive." Just "this thing you can't explain has a name, and it is not a character flaw."
Beats
0-3sthe still frame, the word lands. 3-20syou describe the wall from the inside: the task is easy, you want to do it, and you still cannot make the first move happen. 20-40sname it as task initiation, separate from willpower. 40s+the relief: it is a wiring thing, not a worth thing.
Payoff
"You are not lazy. Your brain just does not hand you the start. That is the part we built around."
On screen
the start is the hard partnot lazinessa wiring thing, not a worth thing
Call
Follow. Soft. "If this is you, there is more here."
Why it works
Users are exhausted by content that talks at them like a problem to optimize. Being seen, with no fix demanded, is the rarest thing in this feed. It earns the follow on trust, not on a tip.
Maddy
Optional and powerful: Maddy says the line in her own words, peer to peer. Her rung, her call.
Assets
📷app: empty list / "just one thing" screen need: a clean screen-recording of the focus screen at low-capacity state

S1 Supporter "Did you do it yet" is the worst thing you can ask. planned ▸
⚡The hook (first 2 seconds)
First frame
A phone screen, close. A text bubble drops in: "did you do it yet??" Then the thumb hesitates, and the screen goes dark.
The draw
The flinch. The buzz, the message, then a real human beat of the screen closing instead of replying. Anyone who has been on either side of that text feels it land.
The line
"If you love someone with ADHD, this text makes it worse."
For
Supporter. The friend, sibling, or coworker who wants to help and keeps reaching for the check-in. In the app their job is to cheer on, not watch over. This video teaches the difference before they ever open it.
Beats
0-3sthe flinch. 3-18swhy checking in backfires: it adds a second task (managing your worry) on top of the first. 18-38swhat helps instead: presence, specifics, and cheering the start, not the finish. 38s+the reframe: support, not surveillance.
Payoff
"You do not have to track them. You just have to be glad to be in it with them. That is the whole job."
On screen
checking in is a second taskcheer the start, not the streaksupport, not surveillance
Call
Follow, plus a soft line: "this is exactly the role Maddy gives people who want to help without hovering."
Why it works
Almost no one makes content for the person on the supporting side who is trying and getting it wrong with love. It converts a guilty helper into an informed one, and it maps one to one onto a real role in the app.
Maddy
Named only. This one is Jeremy to camera, dad to other supporters.
Assets
📷app: Supporter view ("cheering on") 📷app: encouragement / reaction surface need: screen-rec of the outer-ring Supporter home + a reaction being sent
Canon check: the app has no watching-eye surface (no live map, no last-active, no online dot). Keep the video true to that: the win is the supporter doing less watching, not better watching.

P1 Peer The most underrated way to help a friend who cannot start: be in the room. planned ▸
⚡The hook (first 2 seconds)
First frame
Two people on a couch, each on their own thing, not talking. Warm, quiet, ordinary. One soft ambient pulse passes between two dots on screen.
The draw
Calm against a loud feed. While every other video is shouting, this one is still and warm. The contrast itself is the scroll-stopper. Slow push-in, no hard cut.
The line
"Sometimes the help is just being in the room. No pressure. No checking. Just there."
For
Peer. The friend who is not a caregiver and does not want to manage anyone. They just want to be alongside. In the app, that is exactly their role: standing alongside, presence not pressure.
Beats
0-3sthe quiet two-shot. 3-18sname body doubling in plain words: starting is easier when someone is simply present. 18-35swhat it is not: not supervising, not nagging, not performing. 35s+presence is a real form of help, and it costs almost nothing to give.
Payoff
"You do not have to fix anything. Being there, doing your own thing nearby, can be the thing that gets them started."
On screen
presence, not pressurealongside, not overjust be in the room
Call
Follow. "Maddy has a quiet way to be that person, even from far away."
Why it works
It is a pattern interrupt. A calm, warm video in a feed of urgency reads as honest and gets saved and sent to a specific friend, which is the share that actually moves people.
Maddy
Strong fit for Maddy's own voice if she wants it. Peer to peer is literally her register.
Assets
🎥b-roll: two people, parallel quiet activity 📷app: Peer view ("standing alongside") + presence signal need: a warm two-shot clip + screen-rec of the Peer ambient presence surface
Canon check: body doubling is user valued and mechanism plausible, not clinically proven. Frame it as a felt thing that helps, never as a proven treatment. Maddy's version is asynchronous and ambient, which avoids the surveillance trap by design.

These four are the direction, one per audience, including the two roles that had zero coverage before (Supporter and Peer). The full bank rebuilds into this format on your go. Cards 01 and 05 are real keepers from the existing 24; S1 and P1 are new episodes written straight at the outer ring.

How this page works
  1. Every card opens with the hook. First frame, the draw, the line. Build the open from that block, not from words alone.
  2. The badge says who it is for. Filter by audience to batch-film one role at a time. Filter by status to see only what is left.
  3. Close a video out by tapping its status: Planned, Filming, Editing, Posted. It saves on this device instantly.
  4. Keep me in sync. Tap "Copy status for Jeremy's dad" at the bottom, paste it back to me in chat, and I log it in the vault so the page and I always agree. That is the "log it and know it" loop.
  5. Assets show what each video needs. A blue link means the file is in hand. Grey italic means it is still on the shot list.
