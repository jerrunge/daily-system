How wide is the door?

You asked whether AuDHD+ is too narrow. Short answer: AuDHD+ is the right anchor and the wrong ceiling. Keep AuDHD as the true origin and the trust source. Widen the outward audience to the whole executive-function world, governed by the honesty boundary. The "+" stops being "autism plus ADHD" and becomes "and every brain where starting is the wall."

The recommendation in one line

AuDHD-anchored, executive-function-wide. Do not rename or re-found around "everyone." Re-layer: AuDHD stays the spine and the proof; the message, the keywords, the founder content, and the practitioner targets widen to ADHD, autism, TBI and stroke, long COVID, perimenopause, depression, and burnout, all under "the same mechanism, never Maddy is proven to cure you."

01

What the research actually shows

Finding 1: the category leader already made this exact move, and won

Tiimo is the strongest comparable. It was, in its own words, "built with ADHD and Autism in mind," and it has deliberately widened its public positioning to "our tools support executive functioning for every brain... whether you are neurodivergent, self-diagnosed, or just someone who wants planning to feel easier." Its tagline is now "Visual Planner for Every Neurotype." That widening did not cost it credibility: it won iPhone App of the Year at the 2025 App Store Awards, carries an NHS certification, and is past 3 million downloads.

The lesson, and the limit

Broadening from an authentic neurodivergent anchor to an executive-function-universal door is a proven, award-winning move, not a dilution trap, when the anchor stays real. The limit: Tiimo is a visual planner (scheduling, time blindness). Maddy is not a planner. Maddy is daily-living scaffolding plus the Circle of people around you, with a no-failure-state design, built with an AuDHD co-designer. So you are not chasing Tiimo into "better calendar." You are widening a lane Tiimo does not occupy.

Finding 2: the wider universe is enormous, and growing

The population united by "my brain's start button is unreliable" is not a niche. It is one of the largest underserved cognitive markets there is.

Finding 3: one language already unites all of them

This is the part that makes widening safe and cheap: you do not need new messaging for each group. They already share a vocabulary. "Brain fog," "executive dysfunction," "I can't start," "the knowledge is there but access is unreliable," "you are not lazy." Long COVID patients, perimenopausal women, ADHD adults, autistic adults, TBI survivors, and people in depression all reach for the same words. The discourse leaders say it out loud: executive dysfunction is named in the same breath across ADHD, autism, TBI, depression, and PTSD.

Your founder voice is already the universal key

"You are not lazy. Starting is the wall." That line, the spine of the Jeremy track, is not AuDHD-specific. It is the exact thing a long COVID patient, a perimenopausal woman, and a TBI survivor each say about themselves. Founder-forward and audience-wide are the same move: your story is the universal entry point, and it widens the audience for free.

Finding 4: your own canon already licenses this

The research foundation (Part 10) already states, with citations, that Maddy's mechanisms are documented to help executive-function-impaired populations broadly, "including TBI, long COVID, depression, PTSD, chronic illness, and burnout," and that Goal Management Training, the approach behind the task design, has meta-analytic support across TBI, ADHD, MS, and stroke. You are not inventing a new claim. You are choosing to speak to a population the canon already says the mechanism serves. The honesty boundary is what keeps it clean: name the mechanism, never Maddy, as the subject of any cross-population claim.

02

The two real risks (and how to not trip them)

Flattening the ND community

"We are all a little neurodivergent" trivializes the people who are the heart of this, and the AuDHD community has been burned by exactly that kind of erasure. Mitigation: AuDHD stays the specific, named anchor and origin. Breadth is framed as "the same mechanism helps others," never "everyone is neurodivergent" and never "Maddy is for everyone." The "+" welcomes adjacency; it does not dissolve the center.

The generic-productivity trap

Go too wide, too flat, and you become the thing you differentiate against: another productivity app. Mitigation: Maddy's difference is not the audience, it is the design: daily living rather than scheduling, the Circle of support, no failure state, built-with not built-for. Keep leading with those, and "wide audience" never reads as "generic app."

03

The three options, honestly

Option AAuDHD+ as-is, "+" stays quiet.

The current posture. Safe, but it leaves the largest underserved cognitive market on the table and undersells a mechanism your own research says is broad. You are already past this.

Option B   recommendedAuDHD-anchored, executive-function-wide.

Keep the name, the origin, and the AuDHD design truth. Widen the outward message, the App Store keywords, the founder content, and the practitioner targets to the whole executive-function and brain-fog universe. Elevate the "+" from "autism plus ADHD" to the explicit bridge: "AuDHD, and every brain where starting is the wall." Honesty boundary governs every claim. This is the Tiimo move, executed from your more differentiated position, with your founder voice as the universal key.

Option CRe-headline as an executive-function / brain-fog daily-living app, AuDHD as origin story.

Maximum reach. But pre-launch, with no outcome data and no installed base, this trades away the two things currently carrying you: the ND-specific trust that earns practitioner referrals, and the authenticity that makes the founder story land. It also most risks the flattening and generic-app traps. Not now. Hold C as a post-launch evolution if real adjacent-population data comes in (a long COVID user says "this changed my mornings"), at which point you can ratchet up honestly with proof.
