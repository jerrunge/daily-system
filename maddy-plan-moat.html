The moat

The highest-return work in the whole plan. Practitioners who vouch, reached one real relationship at a time, with the drafts already written and signed.

Do this one. The drafts are ready to send.

If video is the spine, this is the moat. Practitioners convert caregivers in a way ads never will, because a parent trusts the OT who knows their kid more than any video. It needs no audience and no budget, just your time and your story. One practitioner can refer across the whole "+" because executive-function support is what they already do. The most return per hour of anything in this plan.

Why it is the moat

  • A caregiver will hand their struggling kid a tool a professional vouched for, long before they trust an app they found in an ad.
  • Practitioners are the original word-of-mouth engine, and word of mouth is the only channel that has converted for you so far.
  • Occupational therapists in particular do executive-function rehab for AuDHD, ADHD, and brain injury. One relationship reaches the whole "+".
  • It compounds. Each practitioner who refers becomes a standing channel, not a one-time view.

The outreach sequence (per practitioner)

  1. Engage first, for real. Follow, watch, comment something specific and human on two or three of their posts over a week. No pitch yet. You are a dad in their world, not a vendor.
  2. Warm DM. Short, founder-voice, no ask beyond a soft "would it be alright to send a one-pager." (Draft below.)
  3. One-pager on yes. A single page: the design rationale, the research foundation, the honesty boundary, what it does and does not claim. (Note on the gate below.)
  4. Offer the reviewer seat. Invite them to look at it before launch. The reviewer becomes the referrer; being asked for their expertise is the conversion.
  5. Stay in relationship. This is warm and slow. One real practitioner relationship beats fifty cold emails.

The one-pager gate, stated honestly

The practitioner one-pager should get an ND-affirming OT pre-publish read before it goes wide. Right now there is no active reviewer (the two prior contacts are both out of play). So the first real win in this section is identifying one new ND-affirming OT reviewer from scratch. The early DMs below can double as that search: the practitioner who agrees to review is often the one who ends up referring.
