Founder-Forward Video: the real plan

A recommendation for video across three tracks (Jeremy, Maddy, the two of you together) plus the mechanisms video alone cannot replace. Benchmarked against what is actually working in the world right now, and honest about what will not work.

01The bet, stated plainly

Maddy's one unfair advantage is that the intimacy is not performed. The category is full of faceless apps doing a warm voice behind a logo. You have a real dad and a real daughter who actually built this together. Founder-forward video is just letting the surface tell the truth the product is already made of.

But be clear-eyed about the shape of this bet:

This is a compounding trust play, not a growth hack

No single video saves this. It works because it is real and because it stacks: a body of honest founder video plus an audience that trusts you becomes the asset that makes practitioners say yes and warm caregivers convert at launch. Every ad dollar resets to zero when you stop. This does not. That is the entire reason to do it, and also the reason it is slow.

What this bet is not: it is not a path to fast numbers, it is not influencer cosplay, and it is not "put the kid on camera and watch it pop." The version that works is patient, founder-led, and unusually careful about Maddy. The version that blows up in your face is the impatient one.

02What is actually working in the world right now

Grounding this against current reality, not theory. Here is the honest read of what performs in exactly your space as of mid-2026.

Short-form story beats production value

The neurodivergent creators who own this audience win on rawness and specificity, not polish. Paige Layle (2.6M on TikTok), Chloe Hayden (1.1M), AJ Wilkerson / Captain Autism Comedy (1.2M), and Morgan Foley (650K) built on conversational, lived-experience video: a single real moment, named precisely, often funny. The lane that converts caregivers is the practitioner-creator lane (CHADD, ADDitude, local hubs like ADHDKC, the Holdernesses' "ADHD is Awesome"), and its native phrase is "scaffolding without nagging." None of them out-produce a studio. They out-specify it.

Founder and build-in-public storytelling earns durable trust, slowly

A founder who built a tool for their own brain is a story this community actively engages with, not an ad it scrolls past. The trade is real: high trust, low velocity. It compounds over months, not days.

Lives are a depth engine, not a discovery engine

Lives deepen an audience that already exists. They do almost nothing to create one, and a live to an empty room is demoralizing and a waste of your scarcest resource (your time and your nervous system). Lives come after the short-form has gathered people, not on day one.

The trap this whole space falls into

Vanity metrics. Your current problem is the proof: thousands of views, almost no signups. Views are not the goal and never were. For a trust-gated product sold to risk-averse buyers, the only numbers that matter are qualified follows, practitioner replies, and waitlist conversion. A video with 1,200 views that brings one OT into a conversation beats a 40,000-view video that brings nothing.

03The consent spine (this goes first, on purpose)

Because Maddy is in this, the framework for how she is in it comes before the creative, not after. This is not caution for its own sake. The thing that makes her involvement powerful (it is real) is the exact thing that breaks if she is even slightly pushed. Run her participation the way the app runs its Circle: opt-in, granular, at the center, revocable.

The choice menu: "in or out" as a real, per-piece decision

Out
Not this one. Always available, no reason needed, costs nothing between you.
Named only
"Co-designed with Maddy." Credit, no likeness, no words.
Her words
Not her face. A line she wrote, a design note in her voice, a voiceover. Her perspective without being on camera.
Her presence
Pre-agreed. She appears in a piece you lead, approved before it ships.
Her lead
She drives it. Her story, her feature, her voice as the AuDHDer it was built with. At 21 this is fully hers to choose, held only to her comfort and the care guardrails.

A young AuDHDer choosing her own level of exposure is also just good autonomy and sensory design. It matches how her brain works.

How the choosing stays safe

  • She sees the finished asset before it goes public, not a description of it. Approving an idea and approving the real thing are different.
  • Two yeses, either can stop it. Hers, and yours as the person protecting both her and the brand. Her no always wins. Your safety concern can pause a piece for a conversation. At 21 the call is ultimately hers; your gate is a check you both agree to, not a veto over an adult.
  • A cooling beat. Nothing she is unsure about ships the same day.
  • Comment shield. Default: she does not read the comments. Someone stands between her and the section. ND and parenting spaces can be cruel.
  • Permanence lens. Content is forever. The test is not only "is she okay with this now" but "would future her be okay that she did this." At 21 that is hers to weigh, alongside you.
  • Masking watch. If a piece makes her perform a self instead of being herself, it is the wrong piece, no matter how well it would do.
  • Logged per piece in JER-384, with the rung she chose and her explicit yes. That log is the integrity backbone.

The consent reality: Maddy is 21, so this is simpler, but not nothing

Maddy is a legal adult. That clears away the part of this that constrains most family content. The child-influencer and minor-content laws (California's Coogan extension, Illinois' trust rule, Minnesota's under-14 restriction) do not apply to her. Her consent is fully hers to give, and fully hers to withdraw.

What being an adult does not change, and what still governs every piece:

  • The care guardrails stand on their own. Comfort, the permanence lens, the masking watch, and the comment shield are not legal requirements you can now skip. They are wellbeing requirements. An adult AuDHDer who is also your daughter and the app's namesake is still stepping into spaces that can be cruel, and that is true at 21 the same as it would be at 15.
  • Permanence still outlives the moment. Twenty-one-year-old Maddy choosing yes is real and sufficient. Thirty-year-old Maddy still inherits whatever ships. "Would future her be glad this exists" stays a live question, now hers to weigh as much as yours.
  • The cultural standard still informs the care, even though the law does not bind here. The broader reckoning about putting family members in monetized content is about consent and dignity, not only age. Holding the high standard anyway is what keeps this authentic.

And the upside of her being 21 is real: she is inside Maddy's own target audience (AuDHD adults), so her voice is not just permissible, it is peer-credible. "Her lead" is fully on the table as her choice, not a risk to be managed. If she ever wants a genuine creator presence of her own, talking to other AuDHD adults as one of them, that is available too. The constraint here is her choice and her comfort, not her age.

04Track: Jeremy the spine

This is the load-bearing track and the one to start with. You, the dad, telling the truth. It carries the brand whether or not Maddy ever appears, which is exactly why it is the foundation: it does not depend on her.

Format

Short-form, 60 to 90 seconds, talking to camera or your voiceover over simple b-roll. Low production on purpose. The currency is honesty and specificity, not lighting. Lead with the problem and your life; the app is the resolution of the story, never the subject of it.

The opening arc (the first 8, in order)

01 / Origin
"My daughter could fly across the world alone but could not start the dishes. Here is what I finally understood."
The capability-versus-performance hook, straight from the research. It stops the scroll because it sounds like a contradiction and resolves into recognition.
02 / The reframe
"If you have watched your kid get called lazy when you know that is not it, this one is for you."
Aimed at the caregiver, not the user. "You are not lazy" is the single most native line in this entire community. This is the caregiver doorway.
03 / The wall
"Starting is the wall. Not the doing, the starting." Task initiation, named.
Names the exact felt experience (task paralysis, staring at the spoon). High save rate, because saving is how people say "this is me."
04 / Why the other apps failed us
"Every app I tried assumed she was disorganized. She is not disorganized. The lists made it worse."
Differentiation without naming competitors. Speaks to every parent who has bought five apps that did not stick.
05 / The Circle
"The goal was never for her to need us forever. The support is designed to fade."
Body doubling is native vocabulary here. Caregiver-fade as a feature, not a bug, is the thing no faceless competitor frames.
06 / The shame loop
"Red rings and 'you failed today' do real harm to these brains. So we built something with no failure state."
Research-grounded, emotionally true, and a clean values statement.
07 / What I got wrong
"Here is a thing I built, watched her not use, and had to tear out."
Build-in-public honesty. This is the episode that makes people trust the other seven.
08 / The wider door
"This started for my autistic and ADHD daughter. Turns out the same wall shows up for a lot of brains."
The "+". Honest at the mechanism level. Opens the audience to ADHD, TBI, and the wider executive-function world without a single overclaim.

The honesty boundary lives here too

Your story can go anywhere. "Maddy is proven to help X" still cannot. Episode 08 says "the same wall shows up for a lot of brains," which is true and yours to say. It does not say "Maddy treats TBI." The honest founder is the credible founder, and that credibility is the whole reason an OT will trust you. Do not trade it for a stronger-sounding line.

Cadence, honestly

Two to three short videos a week is realistic and sustainable for a founder who is also building the thing. Batch them: film several in one sitting, release across the week. Do not promise yourself daily. Daily is how this dies in a month (see "How this fails").

Where

TikTok and Instagram Reels as primary (both in your canon scope). Threads for passive cross-post. The origin (Episode 01) is the candidate for a longer YouTube cut if you reopen that scope.

05Track: Maddy the multiplier

When she opts in, this is the most credible content the brand can possibly make. A young AuDHDer who co-designed the tool, talking to other AuDHDers, is peer trust you cannot buy. To caregivers it does something even rarer: it inverts their fear. Not "your kid is struggling," but "here is a kid like yours who is a designer, who made something real." You are offering hope instead of pity, and a parent will follow hope.

Critical: this is not a channel she is obligated to feed. It is a set of options she picks from, per piece, at her rung. Some months it is nothing. That is success, not failure.

What it looks like at each rung

  • Named "Co-designed with Maddy" appears in your videos. She is credited, present in the story, not on camera.
  • Her words She wrote one of the check-in states; a card carries a design note in her voice (Dancing Script, her exclusive type); she voices a line over your b-roll.
  • Her presence She appears briefly in a piece you lead and approved together.
  • Her lead She explains a feature she asked for, in her own voice. Fully available as her choice, comment-shielded, held to the same care guardrails as everything else. As a 21-year-old AuDHD adult she is a peer to the audience, which makes this the most credible content in the whole plan when she wants it.

The co-design piece is the quiet superpower

"Here is a feature, and here is why I designed it this way," from the person it is for, is marketing and method at the same time. It is visible proof of the one claim no competitor can stage: that this was built with an AuDHDer, not just for an imagined one. Even at the "her words" rung, with no face, this lands.

06Track: Together the heart

The dad-and-daughter co-design conversation is the single most powerful asset available to you. Precisely because it is so strong, use it sparingly. Scarcity is what keeps it real and what protects her.

Forms

  • "She asked for this feature. Here is the conversation we had." (Her at the rung she chooses; can be her words over the story, not necessarily her face.)
  • Designing a small thing together, shown honestly.
  • Her reacting to something you built from her feedback.

Why sparing is non-negotiable

Volume is exactly what tips "a dad and his daughter who built an app" into "a family-content channel," which reads as inauthentic no matter how real it is. A handful of together-pieces a year reads as genuine collaboration. A weekly together-series reads as mining the relationship for content, and it would quietly torch the trust advantage the whole strategy is built on. Restraint is not timidity here. It is the strategy.

07The mechanisms video cannot replace

Video is the trust-builder. It is not the whole machine. Honest about that: a founder who only makes videos and skips these will underperform a founder who makes fewer videos and does these.

the moat

Practitioner outreach, founder-signed

Your highest-leverage move, and it needs no audience and no ad budget. ND-affirming OTs, ADHD coaches, special-ed and ND therapists. Caregivers trust them more than any ad. One OT relationship refers AuDHD, ADHD, and TBI clients at once, because they all do executive-function work. Your founder video does real work here: an OT can watch you tell the story before a call, so trust is half-built before you speak. Start at the @neurodiversity_ot hub and the ND-affirming OT directories.

amplify

Borrowed trust, as collaboration

The AuDHD and parent-advocate creators above will share a founder-built-with-his-daughter story in a way they would never run an ad read. Approach as collaboration, not a paid shout. The co-design truth is the thing they will actually want to pass on.

retain

Lives, once there is a room

After the short-form has gathered people, lives deepen them: office hours, "ask a dad who built this," co-design Q and A. Powerful for the warm middle of the funnel. Pointless before the audience exists.

capture

Word of mouth and the site

Parents are already recommending you by hand; that is the rarest signal you have. Make passing it on frictionless, and fix what a warm caregiver sees when they land on mymaddy.app so it speaks to the buyer, not only the user. You are leaking earned interest if the page only talks to the AuDHDer.

And the plumbing is already yours: the lead-capture and auto-invite pipeline is live. Video drives the follow, the follow becomes a waitlist signup, the pipeline sends the invite. The video tracks feed a machine that already runs.

09How this fails (the part you asked me not to soften)

Chasing one viral hit

There is no single video that saves this. It compounds or it does nothing. If you need a hit to believe it is working, you will quit before it works.

Worshipping views

The exact failure you are living now: big views, no signups. If the dashboard you check is view count, you will optimize for the wrong thing and feel busy while nothing converts.

Founder burnout

Promising daily, doing it for three weeks, resenting it, stopping. Batch and go sustainable, or this becomes another abandoned good idea.

Maddy at the center

Her face converts, which is exactly the temptation. Over-index on it and you risk her wellbeing and the authenticity the whole thing runs on. Restraint protects her and the brand at once, even though at 21 the choice is fully hers.

Lives too early

Going live to nobody, feeling foolish, concluding "video does not work." Video works. The order was wrong.

Going faceless when it gets hard

The first slow month, the pull will be to retreat behind the logo. That retreat throws away the only durable advantage you have. The face is the strategy.

10Metrics that actually matter

Stop counting views. Count these:

  • Qualified follows: caregivers and AuDHDers who engage, not raw reach.
  • Saves and shares over likes: a save means "this is me" or "this is my kid." That is intent.
  • Waitlist signups attributable to video, not impressions.
  • Practitioner replies and relationships opened. One real OT conversation is worth tens of thousands of views.
  • Maddy's comfort, tracked honestly as a success metric, the way caregiver-fade is a success metric inside the app. If it ever competes with a number, it wins.

The one-line version

Lead with Jeremy, the dad who tells the truth. Let Maddy in at the rung she chooses, protected, never at the center. Use the two of you together rarely and powerfully. Point all of it at practitioners and caregivers, not at view counts. Be patient enough to let it compound, and careful enough that it stays real, because real is the entire edge.
